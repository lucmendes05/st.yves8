<p>

I wish it could be this time next Thursday,<br>

live within a coin flip - live within the closing of a curtain, <br>

in the lamina of faith on the eighth floor, <br>

from the balcony next to the room I was born in and 8am has never been so distant; <br>

</p>

     

 <p>      

I will never not be at that party again, <br>

in that classroom again, <br>

every time I close my eyes to take me there again, <br>

to where I was when I was younger and everything was so far away; <br>

 </p>


<p>

I don’t want to bury my grandparents <br>

- if I see a shovel I swear I will bite - <br> 

to be an adult and grow up, <br>

to smoke more often and alone, <br>

not hear the stories of days that died with him and her so soon; <br>

</p>

     

<p>

promise you won’t disappear if I accept this, <br>

tell me that you won’t hold yourself to it, <br>

trust in me and peace will come, <br>

the music will keep playing and the beaches will go nowhere - <br>

there is a balance in the whisper of a word, <br>

in the shaking breath that reads it and the pause which follows; <br>

</p>
